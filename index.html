Why ClaimsIQ exists

Built to make insurance data legible.

15-year cohort 88–98% market coverage NCID verified 1.78M policies

Irish motor insurance generates massive volumes of pricing and claims data, but much of it remains fragmented and difficult to interpret. ClaimsIQ transforms regulator-published NCID data into a structured analytics platform for understanding profitability, risk concentration, claims inflation, and pricing adequacy across the Irish insurance market.

Every figure is traceable to a published Central Bank source. The platform is designed for insurance practitioners, actuaries, pricing analysts, and anyone who needs to understand the forces reshaping the Irish motor market beyond the headline premium number.

Structural shift
Damage vs injury share of total cost
A decade-long structural shift culminated in 2022 when damage overtook injury for the first time, now representing 53% of settled costs. Insurers weighted toward bodily injury reserving are systematically underreserving for repair inflation and parts supply disruption.

Average premium vs claims cost per policy by age band
Young driver segments generate claims costs 40% above the loaded premium, while mid-age cohorts cross-subsidise the book. This creates adverse selection pressure if competitors price more accurately by segment, gradually attracting the low-risk cohorts and leaving the cross-subsidised losses behind.

PIG savings, 2021 to 2023
Annual market saving from guidelines (EUR M)
The Personal Injuries Guidelines represent the most impactful insurance reform in Ireland in a generation, delivering EUR 435M in cumulative savings since April 2021. The critical unfinished business is extending effective guideline application to litigated claims, which still operate under legacy Book of Quantum valuations 40 to 60% higher for equivalent injuries.
Annual PIG savings 2021–2023.
Pricing adequacy
Written vs required premium (70% LR target)
In 2023 the written premium is only EUR 82 above the required level, the tightest margin in 15 years. Any acceleration in claims inflation without a corresponding rate action will push the market into technical under-pricing, triggering a mandatory hardening cycle.

Injury claim cost trend
Average settled injury claim cost with PIG introduction marker, April 2021
Average settled injury costs have continued to rise in nominal terms even after the guidelines took effect, because savings are concentrated in direct and IRB channels while the overall average is dragged upward by litigation costs and legacy pre-guideline cases still working through the courts. The full financial benefit will crystallise as the litigation backlog clears over the next three to five years.
