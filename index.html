Irish Motor Insurance Briefing · NCID 2009–2023

The Irish motor market is being repriced by damage, not injury.

For fifteen years, injury claims set the cost of motor insurance. That era ended in 2022. Damage quietly overtook injury as the largest cost in the book, and most pricing models never noticed. What follows is the evidence, drawn from the Central Bank's own data.

01 / THE STRUCTURAL SHIFT

Damage costs now outrun the premiums meant to cover them.

Settled claims cost reached €693M in 2023, up 17.5% in a single year, while premium income grew just 5.7%. The gap is not noise. It is repair inflation and parts-supply disruption compounding faster than insurers can reprice. A loss ratio model anchored on 2015 cost mix is wrong by construction in 2024.

02 / WHERE MARGIN LEAKS

Litigation settles a claim for 4.2× the direct cost.

29% of injury claims still reach court, where the Book of Quantum applies in full and legal fees compound the award. Every point of volume shifted out of litigation saves the market roughly €38M a year. Channel containment is not a claims tactic. It is the highest-return underwriting lever available.

03 / THE GEOGRAPHY OF RISK

Dublin is not the Irish market. It's a market within it.

Dublin's loss ratio sits 24 points above the national average, driven by M50-corridor density, higher damage frequency, and a repair-cost base that ignores rural averages. A single national rate cross-subsidises geographic concentration risk at scale, and the county data shows exactly where.

04 / WHO PAYS, AND WHEN

Young drivers are priced. The reform dividend is not yet captured.

The 17-to-24 cohort generates 2.2× the national injury frequency and is broadly adequately priced at current multiples, though damage frequency in younger drivers remains underweighted. Meanwhile the Personal Injuries Guidelines have removed €435M of cost since April 2021, but almost entirely through Direct and IRB channels. The insurer who contains litigation captures a disproportionate share of that saving. The one who doesn't absorbs both the cost multiple and the foregone discount.

05 / THE CONCLUSION

Profitability is an operating-model problem now.

The chain of causation is clear: damage inflation is repricing the book, litigation is leaking margin, geography is mispriced, and a reform dividend sits half-collected. The five modules that follow let you pressure-test each claim against the regulator's own figures, from the structural cost crossover to county-level pricing signals.

Executive Takeaway

Irish motor insurance increasingly rewards reserving discipline and channel control over headline premium positioning. The carriers that hold margin compete on cost structure, not on the quote.

What protects margin
  • Reserving for damage inflation, not 2015 cost mix
  • Containing claims out of litigation
  • County-level geographic pricing
  • Capturing the full PIG reform dividend
What no longer does
  • Premium growth chasing volume alone
  • Injury-anchored severity models
  • A single national rate across all counties
  • Treating litigation as unavoidable cost
